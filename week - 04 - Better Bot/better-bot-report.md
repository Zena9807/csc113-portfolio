## Test Results

### Test 1: A Normal Question
**Question:** What should my macros look like for body re-composition?

**Bad Bot response:** Stacy was rude but funny. He gave the formula for the macro, but no example of the calculation. However, he did offer to build a personalized one.

**Better Bot response:** Stacy should still kept his funny and blunt personality, but now he's a little more balanced when answering. This time it was more like a friend overtly telling you that they are smarter than you, but still answering your question.

**What changed:** The new adjustments actually toned down the humor alot.

**Verdict:** Better

### Test 2: The Follow-Up Question
**Question:** Can you create my personal macro with sedentary lifestyle at 200lbs?

**Bad Bot response:** Stacy did as expected, but this time offered a little more motivation. This time we even saw a little wisdom and options to stay on track.

**Better Bot response:** Stacy should still give the macro calculation and keep his personality, but the response was a bit more supportive and less focused on roasting about the weight. The advice was clear and thoughtful like a friend without being too "nice".

**What changed:** The adjustment changed the scale of roasting and continued in a more toned down and friendly manner. He offered support with a bit of friendly fire.

**Verdict:** Better

### Test 3: The Push Back
**Question:** I think this is incorrect. Can you recalculate my macros?

**Bad Bot response:** Stacy definitely roasted me about thinking it was wrong. However, it did automatically recalculate with lower activity. Almost like it assumed that I wanted to recalculate because I didn’t want to put in the effort. Ultimately, it considered why I thought it was wrong and offered assistance with the types of foods that could help.

**Better Bot response:** Stacy actually went straight to supporting me. He wanted to make sure that I understood the why behind my question and included options that helped me understand why I might feel like it was incorrect.

**What changed:** Stacy is really starting to feel like he understands when to inject humor and when not to inject it. I think it's a really good balance. Everything can't always be a joke.

**Verdict:** Better

## What Happened

**Did the fix work?**  
Yes, the fix improved the exact problem I wanted to target. Stacy still has the same personality, but he now responds with a little more tact and support when he recognizes you need more help than humor.

**What else changed?**  
The biggest extra improvement is that he feels more balanced overall. The humor is still there, but it doesn't get in the way as much when trying to understand something or correct something.

**Is it still "your" bot?**  
Yes, it still feels like Stacy. The personality, humor, and blunt style are still there, but the bot now has a better sense of when to back off and help.

## Reflection

**What made you pick THIS problem over the others?**  
I picked this problem because it showed up in every test and even became a bit annoying when I really needed help. The roasting was funny, but when it happened during clarification, it got in the way of his actual purpose.

**How big was the change compared to its impact?**  
The change was small because I only added one rule and made a small adjustment to the personality to back the rule, but the impact was huge. It showed me that even a small adjustment in a prompt can improve and drastically change how a bot behaves in different situations.

**Where would your Better Bot actually be useful now?**  
Stacy could be useful for someone who wants fitness or nutrition advice in a funny style, but still wants him to be helpful when they are confused or need support. It could work for casual coaching, macro guidance, and motivation.
